# Screening package datasheet guidance

## What to decide up front

**1) Screen type and opening size**
- **Coarse screens** (bar racks): 25-75 mm openings, protect downstream equipment
- **Fine screens**: 3-10 mm openings, reduce TSS/BOD loading on downstream processes
- **Micro screens**: 0.5-3 mm openings, for stringent effluent or reuse applications

**2) Cleaning mechanism**
- **Manual rake**: low capital cost, high labor, suitable for small/backup applications
- **Mechanical rake**: front-cleaned or back-cleaned, standard for most municipal plants
- **Continuous belt**: for high-debris or fine-screen applications

**3) Screenings handling**
- Washer-compactor: washes organics, compacts volume, most common municipal choice
- Screw conveyor: transports screenings to container
- Manual removal: small facilities only

## Process data that drives the quote

### Flow basis (must be explicit)
- Average flow (ADWF or annual average)
- Peak hour flow
- Peak day / wet weather flow
- Peaking factors or basis

### Channel hydraulics
- Channel width (per screen channel)
- Approach velocity (design target: typically 0.6-1.2 m/s)
- Upstream and downstream invert elevations
- Available headloss through screen

### Screenings quantity estimates
- Coarse screens: typically 5-15 L/1000 m³
- Fine screens: typically 15-35 L/1000 m³
- Provide existing data if retrofitting

## Mechanical / design interface items that cause scope gaps

- **Differential pressure setpoint** for cleaning initiation
- **Bypass provisions** (manual bar rack, bypass channel)
- **Odor control** requirements (covers, ventilation)
- **Freeze protection** (indoor vs outdoor, heating)
- **Controls scope**: vendor PLC vs integration to plant SCADA

## What to attach with the datasheet

- Site plan / headworks layout
- Hydraulic profile through screening area
- Existing equipment details (if retrofit)
