# Heat Exchangers datasheet guidance

Covers: HX-ST (Shell & Tube), HX-PF (Plate & Frame), HX-AC (Air-Cooled)

## What to decide up front

**1) Heat exchanger type**

| Application | Recommended Type |
|-------------|------------------|
| Sludge heating | Spiral, shell & tube |
| Digester heating | Shell & tube (sludge-to-water) |
| Chemical heating | Plate & frame (clean fluids) |
| Process cooling | Plate & frame, shell & tube |
| Waste heat recovery | Plate & frame |
| Air cooling (no water) | Air-cooled (HX-AC) |
| High pressure | Shell & tube |

**2) Flow arrangement**
- **Counter-current**: highest efficiency
- **Co-current**: temperature limiting
- **Cross-flow**: air-cooled exchangers
- **Multi-pass**: shell & tube optimization

**3) Materials selection**
- **Carbon steel**: clean water, low corrosion
- **Stainless steel (316)**: brackish water, chemicals
- **Titanium**: seawater, high chlorides
- **Specialty alloys**: aggressive chemicals

## Process data that drives the quote

### Thermal duty
- Heat transfer rate (kW)
- Hot side: inlet/outlet temperatures, flow
- Cold side: inlet/outlet temperatures, flow
- LMTD (Log Mean Temperature Difference)

### Fluid properties (both sides)
- Fluid identity
- Flow rate (m³/h or kg/h)
- Inlet temperature
- Outlet temperature
- Specific heat
- Density
- Viscosity
- Fouling potential

### Pressure conditions
- Operating pressure (both sides)
- Design pressure
- Allowable pressure drop

### Fouling factors
- Shell side: 0.00017-0.00035 m²·K/W (typical)
- Tube side: varies by service
- Sludge: high fouling allowance

## Technology-specific guidance

### Shell & Tube (HX-ST)
- TEMA type (e.g., BEM, AES, AEU)
- Tube pitch and layout
- Baffle type and spacing
- Number of passes (shell and tube)
- Tube length, diameter, wall thickness
- Expansion provisions

### Plate & Frame (HX-PF)
- Plate material and thickness
- Gasket material: EPDM, NBR, Viton
- Number of plates
- Port size
- Frame design (single or double pass)
- Cleanability: fully accessible

### Air-Cooled (HX-AC)
- Fin type and density
- Fan type: forced or induced draft
- Number of fans and bays
- Motor power and VFD
- Design ambient temperature
- Noise requirements

## Mechanical / design interface items

- **Piping connections**: nozzle size, orientation
- **Support structure**: saddles, legs, or frame
- **Access for cleaning**: removable bundle, openable frame
- **Insulation**: if temperature control required
- **Instrumentation**: temperature, pressure, flow
- **Spare capacity**: oversizing for fouling

## Design considerations

### For sludge service
- Velocity: maintain turbulence, prevent settling
- Tube diameter: large enough for solids passage
- CIP/cleaning provisions
- Inspection access

### For clean water/chemicals
- Plate & frame often most compact
- Consider gasket compatibility
- Gasketed vs welded plates

### For outdoor installation
- Freeze protection
- Weather enclosure
- Air-cooled fan winterization

## What to attach with the datasheet

- Process flow diagram with temperatures
- Thermal rating calculations
- Fluid properties at operating conditions
- Existing equipment data (if retrofit)
- Site layout showing exchanger location
