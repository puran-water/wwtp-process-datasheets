# Mixers datasheet guidance

Covers: MX-SUB (Submersible), MX-TOPENTRY (Top-Entry), MX-SIDEENTRY (Side-Entry), MX-JET (Jet/Eductor), MX-STATIC (Static)

## What to decide up front

**1) Mixer type selection**

| Application | Tank Type | Recommended |
|-------------|-----------|-------------|
| Anoxic basin | Open concrete | Submersible (MX-SUB) |
| Equalization | Open concrete | Submersible or Jet |
| Chemical storage | Closed tank | Top-entry (MX-TOPENTRY) |
| Large storage | Closed tank | Side-entry (MX-SIDEENTRY) |
| Inline blending | Piping | Static (MX-STATIC) |
| Chemical dilution | Piping | Static or Jet (MX-JET) |
| Digester | Closed tank | Top-entry, gas mixing, or draft tube |

**2) Mixing objective**
- **Solids suspension**: keep solids from settling
- **Blending**: uniform composition
- **Gas dispersion**: dissolve gas into liquid
- **Heat transfer**: temperature uniformity
- **Chemical reaction**: contact time

**3) Submersible mixer installation**
- **Guide rail mounted**: easy removal for service
- **Pedestal mounted**: fixed position
- **Floating/pontoon**: for lagoons

## Process data that drives the quote

### Tank geometry
- Tank shape: rectangular, circular, square
- Dimensions: length × width × depth
- Liquid volume (m³)
- Operating level range

### Mixing requirements
- Mixing intensity: G (s⁻¹) or velocity gradient
- Bulk velocity: m/s at critical points
- Power input: W/m³
- Turnover time

### Fluid properties
- Liquid type
- Solids content (% or mg/L)
- Specific gravity
- Viscosity
- Temperature

### Typical design values

| Application | Power (W/m³) | Velocity (m/s) | G (s⁻¹) |
|-------------|--------------|----------------|---------|
| Flocculation | 5-20 | 0.1-0.3 | 20-80 |
| Anoxic mixing | 5-15 | 0.1-0.3 | 50-100 |
| Equalization | 10-30 | 0.2-0.4 | - |
| Chemical flash mix | 300-1000 | - | 300-1500 |
| Digester | 5-8 | 0.1-0.2 | - |

## Technology-specific guidance

### Submersible Mixers (MX-SUB)
- Propeller type: hydrofoil (high efficiency) or marine
- Thrust per unit: 500-5000 N typical
- Number of units: based on tank geometry
- Orientation: horizontal or angled
- Materials: cast iron, 316 SS, or coated

### Top-Entry Mixers (MX-TOPENTRY)
- Impeller type: hydrofoil, pitched blade, Rushton
- D/T ratio: impeller diameter / tank diameter
- Shaft length and support
- Seal type: lip seal, mechanical, packed gland
- Drive: direct, belt, or gearbox

### Side-Entry Mixers (MX-SIDEENTRY)
- Entry angle optimization
- Nozzle reinforcement
- Mechanical seal (critical)
- Swirl prevention

### Static Mixers (MX-STATIC)
- Pressure drop per element
- Number of elements: typically 4-8 for blending
- Coefficient of variation (CoV) target
- No moving parts, no power

### Jet Mixers (MX-JET)
- Recirculation pump sizing
- Jet velocity and angle
- Number and placement of jets

## Mechanical / design interface items

- **Mounting**: brackets, rails, nozzles, gussets
- **Shaft and seal**: length, diameter, seal type
- **Drive**: motor, gearbox, VFD if variable
- **Electrical**: submersible cable, junction box
- **Lift mechanism**: for submersible mixer service

## What to attach with the datasheet

- Tank drawings with dimensions
- CFD results (if available)
- Process design basis (solids, mixing objective)
- Access and maintenance constraints
- Electrical supply location
