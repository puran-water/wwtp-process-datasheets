# Oil & Grease Separation datasheet guidance

Covers: 101-OGS

## What to decide up front

**1) Separator type**
- **API separator**: large rectangular basin, gravity separation, for refineries/heavy industry
- **CPI (Corrugated Plate Interceptor)**: inclined plates increase surface area, compact
- **DAF**: dissolved air flotation for emulsified oils (see DAF_guidance.md)
- **Hydrocyclone**: centrifugal separation for high-flow, low-residence applications

**2) Oil type and concentration**
- Free oil (>150 μm droplets): gravity separable
- Dispersed oil (20-150 μm): requires coalescing or flotation
- Emulsified oil (<20 μm): requires chemical treatment or DAF

**3) Discharge requirements**
- Sewer discharge limits (typically 100-400 mg/L O&G)
- Surface water discharge (typically <15 mg/L)
- Reuse requirements

## Process data that drives the quote

### Hydraulic design
- Design average flow (m³/h)
- Design peak flow (m³/h)
- Retention time: 15-60 min for API, 5-15 min for CPI

### Influent quality
- Oil & grease concentration (mg/L)
- Free vs emulsified ratio
- Specific gravity of oil phase
- TSS concentration
- Temperature range

### Performance targets
- Effluent O&G concentration (mg/L)
- Free oil removal: typically >90%
- Emulsified oil: varies with treatment

### Stokes Law parameters (for gravity separators)
- Oil droplet size distribution
- Oil specific gravity (typically 0.85-0.95)
- Water temperature (affects viscosity)
- Rise rate: typically 0.5-2.0 m/h

## Mechanical / design interface items

- **Inlet distribution**: diffuser wall, submerged pipe
- **Oil skimmer**: belt, drum, tube, or weir type
- **Sludge collection**: chain/flight, screw, or manual
- **Covers**: odor control, vapor containment (for volatile oils)
- **Oil storage**: collection sump, transfer to holding tank

## What to attach with the datasheet

- Oil characterization (type, SG, droplet size if known)
- Wastewater analysis (O&G, TSS, temperature)
- Permit discharge limits
- Site constraints and available footprint
