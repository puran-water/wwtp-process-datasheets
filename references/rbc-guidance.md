# RBC (Rotating Biological Contactor) datasheet guidance

Covers: 270-RBC

## What to decide up front

**1) Application**
- **Secondary treatment**: BOD removal
- **Nitrification**: ammonia removal (requires low organic loading)
- **Combined**: BOD removal followed by nitrification stages

**2) Media configuration**
- **Standard density**: 120-150 m²/m³, general BOD removal
- **High density**: 180-200 m²/m³, polishing/nitrification

**3) Staging**
- **Single shaft**: small installations
- **Multiple stages**: 3-4 stages typical for complete treatment
- **Parallel trains**: redundancy and turndown

**4) Drive type**
- **Mechanical drive**: motor/gearbox, most common
- **Air drive**: air-lift cups, less maintenance, variable speed

## Process data that drives the quote

### Hydraulic loading
- Design average flow (m³/h)
- Design peak flow (m³/h)
- Hydraulic loading: 0.03-0.08 m³/m²/d typical

### Organic loading
- Influent BOD (mg/L)
- First stage loading: max 0.02-0.03 kg BOD/m²/d (to prevent Beggiatoa)
- Total system loading: 0.005-0.015 kg BOD/m²/d
- Effluent BOD target (mg/L)

### Disc specifications
- Disc diameter: 2-4 m typical
- Shaft length: 3-8 m typical
- Submergence: 35-40% of disc diameter
- Rotational speed: 1-2 RPM typical
- Peripheral velocity: 0.15-0.3 m/s

### Ammonia removal (if required)
- Influent ammonia (mg/L)
- Loading: 0.5-1.5 g NH₃-N/m²/d
- Effluent ammonia target (mg/L)

## Mechanical / design interface items

- **Shaft and bearing design**: critical for reliability
- **Drive system**: motor, gearbox, coupling, VFD
- **Tank design**: concrete or steel, baffles between stages
- **Covers**: required for odor control and weather protection
- **Secondary clarifier**: sizing for biomass separation

## Operations considerations

- Shaft and bearing inspection schedule
- Biofilm thickness monitoring
- First stage overloading symptoms (white/grey growth)
- Seasonal performance variation

## What to attach with the datasheet

- Process design calculations with staging
- Tank layout and dimensions
- Structural requirements for shaft support
- Electrical load requirements
