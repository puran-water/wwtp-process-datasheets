# Anaerobic Digestion datasheet guidance

Covers: 640-DIG, 640-GH

## What to decide up front

**1) Digester type**
- **Conventional (low-rate)**: long HRT, mixed or unmixed, simple
- **High-rate (CSTR)**: heated, mixed, 15-20 day HRT
- **Two-stage**: acid phase + methane phase, improved stability
- **Temperature-phased (TPAD)**: thermo + meso stages

**2) Temperature regime**
- **Mesophilic**: 35-37°C, stable, most common
- **Thermophilic**: 50-57°C, faster, better pathogen kill, more sensitive

**3) Mixing system**
- **Gas recirculation**: compressed digester gas, unconfined or confined
- **Mechanical mixing**: draft tube, impeller
- **Pump recirculation**: external pumping
- **Combination**: gas + pump

**4) Biogas utilization**
- **Flaring only**: waste gas
- **Boiler**: heating, hot water
- **Combined Heat & Power (CHP)**: engine-generator
- **Upgrading**: biomethane for pipeline or vehicle fuel

## Process data that drives the quote

### Sludge feed
- Sludge types: primary, WAS, or mixed
- Feed flow rate (m³/d)
- Feed solids concentration (%)
- Volatile solids content (%)
- Volatile solids loading: 1.6-4.8 kg VS/m³/d

### Digester sizing
- Hydraulic retention time (HRT): 15-30 days
- Active volume (m³)
- Diameter and depth
- Gas storage volume (% of daily production)

### Biogas production
- Specific gas production: 0.8-1.1 m³/kg VS destroyed
- VS destruction: 45-55% typical
- Biogas composition: 55-70% CH₄, 30-45% CO₂
- H₂S content: typically 100-3000 ppm

### Heat balance
- Heat for sludge heating
- Heat losses through walls/cover
- Heat recovery from CHP (if applicable)
- Supplemental heating requirements

## Mechanical / design interface items

- **Digester tank**: steel or concrete, insulated, cover type
- **Cover**: fixed steel, floating, membrane (gas holder)
- **Heating system**: heat exchangers, hot water loop, steam
- **Mixing system**: gas compressors, diffusers, or mixers
- **Feed system**: grinders, pumps, heat exchanger
- **Gas handling**: condensate removal, pressure relief, flame arrestors
- **Safety**: gas detection, pressure/vacuum relief, emergency flare

## Gas holder considerations

- Storage capacity: typically 25-50% of daily production
- Operating pressure: 15-50 mbar
- Gas holder type: membrane, floating, or external sphere
- Pressure control: regulators, blowers

## What to attach with the datasheet

- Sludge characterization (TS, VS, nutrients)
- Mass and energy balance
- Biogas utilization plan
- Site layout showing digester complex
- Permit requirements (Class A/B biosolids if applicable)
