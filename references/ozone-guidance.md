# Ozone System datasheet guidance

Covers: 401-O3, 401-O3D

## What to decide up front

**1) Feed gas**
- **Air-fed**: simpler, lower O₃ concentration (1-3%)
- **LOX-fed**: higher concentration (6-12%), lower power, most common
- **PSA/VPSA oxygen**: on-site O₂ generation, medium-large plants

**2) Application**
- **Disinfection**: primary or supplemental pathogen kill
- **Micropollutant oxidation**: pharmaceuticals, EDCs
- **Taste & odor control**: drinking water
- **Color removal**: industrial/reuse
- **Sludge treatment**: solubilization for digestion

**3) Contactor type**
- **Fine bubble diffusion**: deep tanks, high transfer efficiency
- **Sidestream injection**: venturi or static mixer, compact
- **U-tube**: very high efficiency, specialized

**4) Destruct system**
- **Thermal catalytic**: most common, heated catalyst
- **Thermal only**: high temperature destruction
- **Catalytic ambient**: emerging technology

## Process data that drives the quote

### Hydraulic design
- Design average flow (m³/h)
- Design peak flow (m³/h)
- Contact time: 5-15 minutes typical

### Ozone demand
- Applied dose: 2-10 mg/L for disinfection
- Transferred dose
- Transfer efficiency: 85-95% for fine bubble
- Residual target (if any)

### Disinfection requirements
- CT requirement (mg·min/L)
- Log reduction required
- Permit compliance point

### Generator sizing
- Production capacity (kg O₃/h)
- Turndown range: typically 10-100%
- Power consumption: 8-12 kWh/kg O₃ (LOX), 15-20 kWh/kg (air)
- Cooling water requirements

### For LOX systems
- LOX consumption: ~10 kg O₂ per kg O₃
- LOX storage: typically 14-30 day supply
- Vaporizer sizing

## Mechanical / design interface items

- **Generator**: corona discharge, water-cooled electrodes
- **Power supply unit (PSU)**: medium frequency, variable output
- **LOX storage**: vacuum-insulated tank, vaporizer
- **Feed gas preparation**: filters, dryers (for air-fed)
- **Contactor**: concrete or SS, diffusers, off-gas collection
- **Destruct unit**: catalyst, heater, exhaust
- **Instrumentation**: O₃ in gas, dissolved O₃, off-gas monitors

## Safety considerations

- Ozone toxicity: 0.1 ppm exposure limit
- Ambient O₃ monitors with alarms
- Ventilation: 6-12 air changes/hour
- LOX: cryogenic hazards, O₂-enriched atmosphere
- Emergency shutdown systems

## What to attach with the datasheet

- Water quality analysis (demand constituents)
- Contactor dimensions and configuration
- CT calculation worksheet
- Electrical load and cooling water availability
- Off-gas handling requirements
