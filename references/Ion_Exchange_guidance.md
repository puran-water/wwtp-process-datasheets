# Ion Exchange datasheet guidance

Covers: 330-IX

## What to decide up front

**1) Target application**
- **Softening**: Ca/Mg removal (SAC Na-form)
- **Dealkalization**: bicarbonate removal (WAC H-form)
- **Demineralization**: complete TDS removal (cation + anion)
- **Selective removal**: nitrate, perchlorate, arsenic, heavy metals
- **PFAS removal**: single-use or regenerable specialty resins

**2) Resin type**
- **SAC (Strong Acid Cation)**: Na+ or H+ form, removes all cations
- **WAC (Weak Acid Cation)**: H+ form, removes hardness with alkalinity
- **SBA (Strong Base Anion)**: OH- or Cl- form, removes all anions
- **WBA (Weak Base Anion)**: removes strong acids only
- **Specialty/selective**: nitrate-selective, PFAS-specific

**3) Regeneration approach**
- **Co-current**: regenerant same direction as service, simpler
- **Counter-current**: better efficiency, more complex
- **Packed bed**: no freeboard, highest efficiency
- **Single-use**: for PFAS or hazardous contaminants

## Process data that drives the quote

### Hydraulic design
- Design average flow (m³/h)
- Design peak flow (m³/h)
- Service flow rate: 10-40 BV/h typical
- Regeneration frequency

### Feed water quality
- TDS and ionic composition (cations/anions)
- Hardness, alkalinity (for softening)
- Target ion concentrations
- TSS: must be <5 mg/L (pre-filter required)
- Chlorine: must be <0.1 mg/L (carbon pretreatment)

### Design parameters
- Resin volume required (L or m³)
- Exchange capacity: meq/L or kg as CaCO₃/m³
- Leakage targets (for demineralization)
- Run length: bed volumes or operating hours

### Regeneration requirements
- Regenerant type: NaCl, HCl, H₂SO₄, NaOH
- Regenerant concentration
- Regenerant dose: g/L resin or lb/ft³
- Rinse water volume

## Mechanical / design interface items

- **Vessel sizing**: diameter, bed depth, freeboard
- **Vessel construction**: FRP (most common), rubber-lined steel, SS
- **Internals**: distributors, collectors, resin retention
- **Regeneration system**: chemical storage, dilution, metering
- **Waste neutralization**: for acid/caustic regenerants
- **Instrumentation**: flow, pressure, conductivity, hardness

## Operations considerations

- Resin life: 5-10 years with proper operation
- Fouling: iron, organics, chlorine damage
- Regenerant waste disposal
- Resin sampling and testing

## What to attach with the datasheet

- Complete water analysis (cations, anions, silica)
- Target water quality specifications
- Regenerant chemical availability
- Waste discharge limits
