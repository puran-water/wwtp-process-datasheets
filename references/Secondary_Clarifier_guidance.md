# Secondary clarifier datasheet guidance

## What to decide up front

**1) Clarifier type**
- **Circular**: center-feed with rotating suction mechanism, most common
- **Rectangular**: chain-and-flight, used where space is constrained

**2) RAS withdrawal**
- **Suction header**: rotating suction tubes remove sludge across floor
- **Individual pumps**: airlift or submersible pumps, simpler but less uniform

**3) Sludge blanket management**
- Blanket level sensors
- RAS flow pacing (flow-proportional or blanket-based)
- SVI monitoring

## Process data that drives the quote

### Flow basis
- Average flow (excluding RAS)
- Peak flow
- Number of clarifiers and redundancy
- RAS ratio range: typically 50-150% of influent

### Biological loading
- Design MLSS from aeration tank
- Expected SVI (sludge volume index): 80-150 mL/g typical
- Design solids loading rate: 100-150 kg/m²/day at average

### Hydraulic loading
- Surface loading rate: 16-28 m³/m²/day at average
- Peak surface loading: up to 50 m³/m²/day
- Weir loading rate: <250 m³/m/day

### Sludge blanket
- Target blanket depth: 0.5-1.5 m
- Blanket level monitoring type

## Mechanical / design interface items

- **Density current baffle**: Stamford baffle or similar, prevents short-circuiting
- **Center well / energy dissipating inlet**: critical for settling
- **Scraper speed**: very slow (1 revolution per 15-30 minutes)
- **Scum removal**: typically required
- **Weir type**: V-notch or flat, often with baffles

## RAS/WAS system
- RAS pumps: capacity, type, VFD control
- WAS withdrawal point: from RAS line or separate
- WAS destination: thickener, digester, etc.

## What to attach with the datasheet

- Process flow diagram showing clarifier connections
- Aeration tank MLSS and SVI data (if existing plant)
- Site layout with clarifier locations
- Hydraulic profile
