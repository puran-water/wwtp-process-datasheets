# Chlorination System datasheet guidance

Covers: 401-CL

## What to decide up front

**1) Chlorine form**
- **Gas chlorine (Cl₂)**: lowest cost, highest hazard, large plants
- **Sodium hypochlorite (NaOCl)**: 12-15% solution, most common
- **On-site generation (OSG)**: 0.8% NaOCl from salt, eliminates delivery
- **Calcium hypochlorite (Ca(OCl)₂)**: tablets/granules, small systems
- **Chlorine dioxide (ClO₂)**: for taste/odor, DBP control

**2) Application point**
- **Pre-chlorination**: upstream, pathogen/algae control
- **Post-chlorination**: after filtration, primary disinfection
- **Chloramination**: combined with ammonia for distribution residual

**3) Dechlorination (if required)**
- **Sulfur dioxide gas**: for gas chlorine plants
- **Sodium bisulfite solution**: most common
- **Sodium thiosulfate**: alternative
- **Activated carbon**: passive removal

## Process data that drives the quote

### Hydraulic design
- Design average flow (m³/h)
- Design peak flow (m³/h)
- Contact time available (minutes)
- Contact tank baffling factor

### Disinfection requirements
- Target organism and log reduction
- Permit residual limits
- CT requirement (mg·min/L)
- Dose: typically 2-10 mg/L for wastewater

### For sodium hypochlorite
- Solution strength: 12-15% as delivered
- Degradation rate: 0.5-1%/week
- Storage volume: typically 30-day supply
- Dilution requirements

### For on-site generation
- Salt consumption: ~3 kg NaCl per kg Cl₂ equivalent
- Power consumption: ~4 kWh per kg Cl₂
- Production rate (kg/day)
- Storage capacity

### Chemical consumption
- Average dose (mg/L as Cl₃)
- Peak dose
- Annual consumption (kg or L)

## Mechanical / design interface items

- **Chemical storage**: tank material (HDPE, FRP), containment
- **Metering pumps**: diaphragm type, capacity, materials
- **Injection point**: diffuser, static mixer, or eductor
- **Analyzer/controller**: residual analyzer, flow-pacing
- **Safety systems**: leak detection, scrubber (for gas), ventilation
- **Contact tank**: baffling, mixing, sampling points

## Safety considerations

- Chlorine gas: scrubber, leak detectors, SCBA, emergency response
- Hypochlorite: incompatibility with acids, ventilation
- Secondary containment: 110% of largest tank
- Emergency shutoff and alarms

## What to attach with the datasheet

- Contact tank dimensions and baffling
- Permit disinfection requirements
- Existing chemical feed systems (if retrofit)
- Safety and emergency response plan
