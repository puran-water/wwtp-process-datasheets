# MBR (Membrane Bioreactor) datasheet guidance

Covers: 250-MBR (submerged), 250-XMBR (crossflow)

## What to decide up front

**1) MBR configuration**
- **Submerged/immersed**: membranes in bioreactor or separate tank, most common
- **Sidestream/crossflow**: external membrane modules, higher energy, easier maintenance
- **Hybrid**: combination approaches

**2) Membrane type**
- **Hollow fiber**: high packing density, backwashable, most common
- **Flat sheet**: lower fouling, easier inspection, higher cost
- **Tubular**: for high-solids or industrial applications

**3) Membrane material**
- **PVDF**: chemical resistant, most common
- **PES/PAN**: lower cost alternatives
- **Ceramic**: extreme durability, high cost

**4) Pore size**
- **Microfiltration (MF)**: 0.1-0.4 μm, standard for wastewater
- **Ultrafiltration (UF)**: 0.01-0.1 μm, virus removal

## Process data that drives the quote

### Biological design
- Design average flow (m³/h)
- Design peak flow (m³/h)
- BOD/COD loading (kg/d)
- TN/TP requirements
- MLSS target: typically 8,000-15,000 mg/L
- SRT: typically 15-25 days

### Membrane sizing
- Net flux: 15-25 LMH (L/m²/h) typical for municipal
- Gross flux: accounts for backwash/relaxation
- Membrane area required (m²)
- Number of modules/cassettes

### Operating parameters
- TMP (transmembrane pressure): typically 10-50 kPa
- Permeability target (LMH/kPa)
- Air scour rate: 0.3-0.6 Nm³/h per m² membrane

### Cleaning requirements
- Maintenance clean frequency (weekly)
- Recovery clean frequency (monthly/quarterly)
- Chemical types: NaOCl, citric acid, NaOH

## Mechanical / design interface items

- **Membrane tank**: sizing, aeration grid, membrane support
- **Air scour blowers**: dedicated or shared with process air
- **Permeate pumps**: suction-side design, flow control
- **CIP system**: chemical storage, dosing, recirculation
- **Pretreatment**: fine screening (1-3 mm mandatory)

## Operations considerations

- Membrane replacement life: 7-10 years typical
- Spare membrane inventory
- Operator training requirements
- Remote monitoring/SCADA integration

## What to attach with the datasheet

- Process design basis (flows, loads, effluent limits)
- Wastewater characterization
- Site layout and tank dimensions
- Electrical load requirements
