# DAF (Dissolved Air Flotation) datasheet guidance

Covers: 130-DAF, 130-SAT

## What to decide up front

**1) DAF application**
- **Primary clarification**: TSS/FOG removal from raw wastewater
- **Secondary clarification**: thickening activated sludge (DAF thickener)
- **Tertiary polishing**: algae/residual TSS removal
- **Industrial pretreatment**: oil/grease removal

**2) Tank configuration**
- **Rectangular**: most common, multiple cells possible
- **Circular**: smaller footprint, single unit
- **Stacked/multi-level**: space-constrained sites

**3) Saturation system**
- **Full-flow pressurization**: entire influent saturated (rare)
- **Partial-flow pressurization**: portion of influent (10-20%)
- **Recycle pressurization**: effluent recycled through saturator (most common, 20-50%)

## Process data that drives the quote

### Hydraulic loading
- Design average flow (m³/h)
- Design peak flow (m³/h)
- Hydraulic loading rate: 5-15 m³/m²/h typical

### Influent quality
- TSS concentration (mg/L)
- FOG/oil concentration (mg/L)
- Temperature range (affects air solubility)

### Performance targets
- TSS removal: typically 85-95%
- FOG removal: typically 90-99%
- Effluent TSS target (mg/L)

### Air/solids ratio
- Typical: 0.02-0.06 kg air/kg solids
- Recycle ratio: 20-50% of influent flow
- Saturator pressure: 400-600 kPa

### Chemical addition
- Coagulant type and dose (if required)
- Polymer type and dose
- pH adjustment requirements

## Mechanical / design interface items

- **Saturator sizing**: pressure vessel with packing or spray nozzles
- **Recycle pumps**: sized for recycle ratio, pressure rating
- **Skimmer mechanism**: chain/flight, rotary, or screw type
- **Sludge/float removal**: beach, trough collection, or screw conveyor
- **Controls**: level control, pressure control, recycle flow

## What to attach with the datasheet

- Influent characterization data (TSS, FOG, temperature)
- Jar test results (if available)
- Site layout and hydraulic profile
- Downstream process requirements
