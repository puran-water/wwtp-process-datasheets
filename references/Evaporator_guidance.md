# Evaporator System datasheet guidance

Covers: 501-MEE (Multi-Effect), 501-MVR (Mechanical Vapor Recompression)

## What to decide up front

**1) Evaporator type**
- **MVR (Mechanical Vapor Recompression)**: single effect, lowest energy, electrical
- **MEE (Multi-Effect Evaporator)**: multiple stages, uses steam
- **TVR (Thermal Vapor Recompression)**: steam ejector, intermediate efficiency
- **Brine concentrator**: forced circulation for high TDS

**2) Number of effects (MEE)**
- Single effect: baseline, GOR = 1
- Multiple effects: 2-7 typical, GOR increases with effects
- Economics: balance capital vs energy cost

**3) Evaporator configuration**
- **Falling film**: high heat transfer, clean feeds
- **Rising film**: viscous feeds
- **Forced circulation**: scaling/fouling feeds, crystallization
- **Horizontal tube**: specific applications

**4) Final concentrate handling**
- **Liquid disposal**: deep well, further treatment
- **Crystallization**: zero liquid discharge
- **Spray dryer**: solid product

## Process data that drives the quote

### Feed characteristics
- Feed flow rate (m³/h)
- Feed TDS (mg/L or %)
- Feed temperature
- Scaling constituents: Ca, Mg, silica, sulfate
- Organic content (COD/BOD)
- pH and corrosivity

### Concentration targets
- Distillate flow rate
- Distillate quality (TDS limit)
- Concentrate TDS target (%)
- Concentration ratio

### Energy requirements
- **MVR**: 15-40 kWh/m³ distillate typical
- **MEE**: 40-80 kg steam/m³ distillate per effect
- Steam pressure available (for MEE/TVR)
- Electrical supply (for MVR)

### Heat transfer
- Overall heat transfer coefficient
- Evaporator temperature
- Temperature difference (ΔT) per effect
- Boiling point elevation

## Mechanical / design interface items

- **Heat exchangers**: falling film, forced circulation, materials
- **Vapor compressor (MVR)**: centrifugal or roots blower
- **Circulation pumps**: high-head, abrasion-resistant
- **Condensate system**: distillate collection, vacuum system
- **Concentrate handling**: pumps, tanks, crystallizer interface
- **CIP system**: acid and caustic cleaning

## Scaling and fouling

- Seed crystal management (if crystallizing)
- Anti-scalant dosing
- Cleaning frequency
- Scale monitoring

## What to attach with the datasheet

- Complete feed water analysis
- Solubility curves for major salts
- Energy availability (steam, power)
- Concentrate disposal plan
- Space and structural constraints
