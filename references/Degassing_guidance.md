# Degassing datasheet guidance

Covers: 340-DEG

## What to decide up front

**1) Target gas**
- **CO₂ removal**: after cation exchange or RO, raise pH
- **O₂ removal**: boiler feedwater, corrosion prevention
- **H₂S removal**: groundwater treatment
- **Radon removal**: drinking water
- **General dissolved gases**: multiple gases

**2) Degasser type**
- **Packed tower (air stripping)**: most common for CO₂, uses air
- **Vacuum tower**: lower temperature operation, no air contact
- **Membrane degasser**: compact, no air contact, for O₂/CO₂
- **Forced draft aerator**: simple, lower efficiency
- **Spray aerator**: lowest cost, lowest efficiency

**3) Air source (for air strippers)**
- **Forced draft**: blower pushes air up through packing
- **Induced draft**: fan pulls air through (top-mounted)
- **Natural draft**: chimney effect, limited application

## Process data that drives the quote

### Hydraulic design
- Design average flow (m³/h)
- Design peak flow (m³/h)
- Air-to-water ratio: 15-30:1 typical for CO₂
- Hydraulic loading: 30-80 m³/m²/h

### Gas removal requirements
- Inlet gas concentration (mg/L)
- Outlet gas concentration target
- Removal efficiency: typically 90-99% for CO₂

### Tower sizing
- Packing depth: 2-5 m typical
- Tower diameter
- Packing type: random (Pall rings) or structured
- Pressure drop allowance

### For vacuum degassing
- Operating pressure (kPa absolute)
- Vacuum pump sizing
- Temperature constraints

### For membrane degassing
- Membrane contactor type
- Sweep gas requirements
- Number of stages

## Mechanical / design interface items

- **Tower construction**: FRP (most common), SS, or concrete
- **Packing support**: grating and support plate
- **Air distribution**: plenum, nozzles
- **Blower/fan**: sizing, materials, VFD
- **Water distribution**: spray nozzles or trough
- **Mist eliminator**: prevent water carryover
- **Sump and transfer pump**: if not gravity flow

## Operations considerations

- Packing fouling (biological, scaling)
- Blower maintenance
- Seasonal temperature effects
- Noise control (blowers)

## What to attach with the datasheet

- Water quality analysis (dissolved gases, temperature)
- Target water quality
- Site constraints (noise, height limitations)
- Downstream process requirements
