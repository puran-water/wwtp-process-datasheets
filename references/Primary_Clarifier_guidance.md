# Primary clarifier datasheet guidance

## What to decide up front

**1) Clarifier geometry**
- **Circular**: most common, center-feed or peripheral-feed, rotating scraper
- **Rectangular**: chain-and-flight scraper, often used with multiple tanks in parallel

**2) Scraper type**
- **Rotating (center-drive)**: circular tanks, standard design
- **Chain-and-flight**: rectangular tanks, moves sludge to hopper

**3) Scum removal**
- Required for municipal wastewater (grease, floatables)
- Skimmer type: rotating, full-radius trough, or ducking skimmer

## Process data that drives the quote

### Flow basis
- Average flow (basis: ADWF, annual average, etc.)
- Peak flow (peak hour or peak day)
- Number of clarifiers and redundancy (N, N+1, 2×100%, etc.)

### Design criteria
- HRT: typically 1.5-2.5 hours at average flow
- Surface loading rate: 30-50 m³/m²/day at average, up to 80-120 at peak
- Weir loading rate: typically <250 m³/m/day

### Influent characteristics
- TSS concentration (mg/L)
- Expected removal efficiency: 50-70% TSS, 25-35% BOD

### Sludge handling
- Primary sludge destination (thickener, digester, etc.)
- Target sludge concentration: typically 3-6% TS
- Sludge pumping frequency and rate

## Mechanical / design interface items

- **Floor slope**: typically 1:12 for circular, steeper for rectangular hoppers
- **Side water depth**: typically 3-4.5 m
- **Scum handling**: pump, trough, disposal destination
- **Odor control**: covers required?
- **Materials**: concrete tank, steel mechanism, weir material

## What to attach with the datasheet

- Site plan with clarifier location
- Hydraulic profile (influent and effluent elevations)
- Sludge handling P&ID or description
