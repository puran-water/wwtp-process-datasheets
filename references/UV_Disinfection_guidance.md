# UV disinfection datasheet guidance

## What to decide up front

**1) Channel configuration**
- **Open channel**: most common for wastewater, gravity flow, modular banks
- **Closed vessel**: pressurized, used for drinking water or reuse applications

**2) Lamp type**
- **Low-pressure (LP)**: monochromatic 254 nm, high efficiency, standard for wastewater
- **Low-pressure high-output (LPHO)**: higher intensity LP lamps
- **Medium-pressure (MP)**: polychromatic, fewer lamps, shorter life, for challenging water
- **LED**: emerging technology, longer life, specific wavelengths

**3) Cleaning system**
- **Manual**: lowest cost, highest labor
- **Mechanical wiper**: automatic, most common for wastewater
- **Chemical cleaning**: citric acid or similar, combined with mechanical

## Process data that drives the quote

### Flow basis
- Average flow
- Peak flow (critical for sizing)
- Minimum flow (turndown for dose control)

### Water quality
- **UVT (UV transmittance)**: critical parameter, typically 55-75% for secondary effluent
- TSS: affects UVT and lamp fouling
- Iron/manganese: cause lamp sleeve fouling
- Hardness: scaling potential

### Disinfection requirements
- Target organism: E. coli, fecal coliform, enterococci, etc.
- Required log reduction: typically 3-4 log
- Permit limit (CFU/100 mL or MPN/100 mL)
- Design UV dose: typically 30-40 mJ/cm² for secondary effluent

## Mechanical / design interface items

- **Level control**: downstream weir gate, critical for consistent dose
- **Lamp redundancy**: typically N+1 banks or 100% standby capacity
- **Power supply**: voltage, frequency, backup power requirements
- **Validation**: bioassay-validated systems required for most permits

## Operations considerations

- Lamp replacement frequency: 8,000-14,000 hours typical
- Sleeve cleaning frequency
- Intensity monitoring and alarm setpoints
- Ballast/driver location and cooling

## What to attach with the datasheet

- Water quality data (UVT, TSS, Fe, hardness)
- Permit requirements for disinfection
- Channel dimensions and hydraulic profile
- Existing disinfection system details (if retrofit)
