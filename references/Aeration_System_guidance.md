# Aeration system datasheet guidance

## What to decide up front

**1) Aeration technology**
- **Fine bubble diffusers**: highest oxygen transfer efficiency (SOTE 25-35%), standard for municipal
- **Coarse bubble diffusers**: lower efficiency, used for mixing or high-fouling applications
- **Mechanical aerators**: surface aerators, brush aerators (oxidation ditches)

**2) Basin configuration**
- Single complete-mix basin
- Plug-flow (series of zones)
- Parallel trains
- Step-feed or contact stabilization

**3) DO control strategy**
- Fixed blower output (simple, less efficient)
- DO-based modulation (most-open-valve, PID control)
- Ammonia-based aeration control (advanced)

## Process data that drives the quote

### Biological process design
- SRT (sludge retention time): 5-15 days typical
- MLSS target: 2,000-4,000 mg/L
- F/M ratio: 0.05-0.15 kg BOD/kg MLSS/day
- DO setpoint: typically 1.5-2.5 mg/L

### Oxygen demand
- Carbonaceous oxygen demand (BOD removal)
- Nitrogenous oxygen demand (if nitrifying)
- Denitrification credit (if applicable)
- Peak-to-average ratio

### Air system sizing
- Standard oxygen transfer rate (SOTR) required
- Alpha factor (process water vs clean water)
- Beta factor (dissolved solids effect)
- Design air flow (SCFM or Nm³/h)

### Blower requirements
- Type: positive displacement, multistage centrifugal, turbo/high-speed
- Number of blowers and redundancy
- Turndown capability
- Inlet air temperature range

## Mechanical / design interface items

- **Diffuser layout**: grid pattern, density, air distribution
- **Air piping**: header sizing, condensate drains, isolation valves
- **Blower building**: HVAC, noise, inlet filters, VFDs
- **Controls**: blower control panel, DO analyzers, SCADA integration

## What to attach with the datasheet

- Process design basis / mass balance
- Basin layout drawings
- Blower building location and constraints
- Electrical service available
