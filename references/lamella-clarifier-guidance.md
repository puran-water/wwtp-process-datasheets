# Lamella clarifier (inclined plate settler) datasheet guidance

> **Note:** Lamella clarifiers (inclined plate settlers) are **distinct from conventional primary clarifiers**. Primary clarifiers are large sedimentation tanks with rotating scrapers for gravity settling of raw wastewater. Lamella clarifiers use inclined plates to increase effective settling area in a compact footprint and are typically used for chemical precipitation or tertiary polishing. Use the appropriate template for your application.

## Purpose

Lamella systems are typically quoted either as:
- **packaged units** (tank + plate packs + sludge hopper + controls), or
- **retrofit plate packs** into a civil basin.

Your datasheet should make that boundary unambiguous.

## Process items that determine sizing and pricing

### Hydraulic loading
Vendors size lamella capacity based on **projected settling area** and a design **surface overflow / rise rate**.

You should provide:
- average and peak flow to the lamella stage
- number of units/trains and turndown expectations
- available headloss / elevations if gravity-fed

### Solids and chemistry
Provide as much of the following as is credible:
- influent TSS (mg/L) and estimated variability
- particle size: fine vs coarse, metal hydroxide precipitation vs biological floc, etc.
- temperature range (viscosity matters)
- pH, alkalinity, and key dissolved metals if precipitating

If chemical treatment is used, include:
- coagulant type and dose range (or “vendor to recommend”)
- polymer type (cationic/anionic/nonionic) and dose range
- presence/absence of flocculation upstream (and detention time)

### Performance targets
Define the constraint that matters:
- maximum effluent TSS or turbidity
- maximum carryover under peak flow
- sludge concentration or blowdown frequency targets (if relevant)

## Mechanical and scope clarifications

- **Sludge removal philosophy**
  - continuous vs intermittent blowdown
  - sludge pumps by vendor or by others
  - expected sludge % solids and destination (to thickener, dewatering, etc.)

- **Scum / floatables**
  - do you need scum baffles, skimmers, or is scum negligible?

- **Materials and corrosion**
  - tank material (coated carbon steel, stainless, FRP)
  - plate material (PVC/PP/FRP) and compatibility with chemicals/temperature

- **Access and maintenance**
  - removable plate packs? washdown provisions?
  - access platforms, ladders, guardrails

- **Instrumentation**
  - flow measurement, turbidity, sludge blanket, level
  - local vs remote I/O and comms protocol

## Critical interface data (often missing)

- Footprint constraints and maximum transport dimensions/weights
- Indoor/outdoor and freeze protection expectations
- Required codes (structural, seismic, electrical classification)
