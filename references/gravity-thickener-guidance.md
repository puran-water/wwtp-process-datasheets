# Gravity thickener datasheet guidance

## What to decide up front

**1) Sludge type**
- **Primary sludge**: thickens well by gravity (4-6% achievable)
- **WAS (waste activated sludge)**: poor gravity thickening (2-3%), often use DAF or belt thickener instead
- **Combined primary + WAS**: intermediate performance

**2) Thickener configuration**
- **Conventional gravity thickener**: circular tank with slow rake
- **Picket fence thickener**: vertical pickets on rake arms, improves solids release
- **High-rate thickener**: inclined plates or tubes, smaller footprint

**3) Cover and odor control**
- Open: simplest, lowest cost
- Fixed cover: odor containment, may need ventilation
- Floating cover: gas collection (if co-thickening with digestate)

## Process data that drives the quote

### Sludge characteristics
- Sludge type (primary, WAS, combined, or other)
- Feed concentration: typically 1-3% TS for primary, 0.5-1.5% for WAS
- Feed flow rate (m³/day or GPD)
- Solids loading (dry tons/day)

### Design criteria
- Solids loading rate:
  - Primary sludge: 90-150 kg/m²/day (20-30 lb/ft²/day)
  - WAS: 25-50 kg/m²/day (5-10 lb/ft²/day)
  - Combined: intermediate
- Hydraulic loading rate: typically 20-30 m³/m²/day
- HRT: typically 24 hours

### Target performance
- Thickened sludge concentration:
  - Primary: 4-6% TS
  - WAS: 2-3% TS (gravity thickening not recommended for WAS alone)
  - Combined: 3-5% TS
- Solids capture: 80-90%

## Mechanical / design interface items

- **Feed well**: distributes sludge evenly, dissipates energy
- **Rake drive**: center or peripheral, very slow speed (0.01-0.03 rpm)
- **Picket fence**: improves gas release and thickening for biological sludges
- **Supernatant withdrawal**: weir overflow or adjustable decant
- **Thickened sludge withdrawal**: gravity or pumped

## Odor and gas considerations

- Primary sludge: potential for septicity and odor
- WAS: may release nitrogen gas during thickening
- Cover ventilation and odor control requirements
- Potential for explosive atmosphere (if combined with digested sludge)

## What to attach with the datasheet

- Sludge production estimates (mass balance)
- Existing sludge data (concentration, volume)
- Site plan with thickener location
- Downstream sludge handling process
