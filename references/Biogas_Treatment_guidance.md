# Biogas Treatment datasheet guidance

Covers: 701-CND (Condenser), 710-FE (Iron Sponge), 710-BIO (Biological Scrubber), 730-CMP (Compressor), 740-MEM (Membrane Upgrading), 750-FLR (Flare)

## What to decide up front

**1) End use for biogas**
- **Flaring**: waste, minimal treatment
- **Boiler fuel**: moisture + H₂S removal
- **CHP (engine/turbine)**: moisture + H₂S + siloxane removal
- **Pipeline injection**: full upgrading to biomethane (>95% CH₄)
- **Vehicle fuel (CNG/LNG)**: upgrading + compression/liquefaction

**2) Treatment train (typical sequence)**
1. Moisture removal (condenser/chiller)
2. H₂S removal (iron sponge, biological, chemical)
3. Siloxane removal (activated carbon, if landfill gas or WWTP)
4. CO₂ removal/upgrading (if biomethane required)
5. Compression (to end use pressure)

**3) H₂S removal technology**
- **Iron sponge/iron oxide**: simple, batch replacement
- **Biological scrubber**: continuous, low operating cost
- **Chemical scrubbing**: NaOH, caustic regeneration
- **Activated carbon**: impregnated, also removes siloxanes

## Process data that drives the quote

### Biogas flow and composition
- Gas flow rate (Nm³/h)
- Methane content (%)
- CO₂ content (%)
- H₂S concentration (ppm)
- Moisture (saturated at digester temp)
- Siloxanes (mg/m³) - if applicable
- Other contaminants (VOCs, ammonia)

### Moisture removal
- Inlet gas temperature
- Dewpoint target: typically 5-10°C
- Condensate flow rate

### H₂S removal
- Inlet H₂S (ppm)
- Outlet H₂S target: <200 ppm for engines, <4 ppm for pipeline
- Media consumption rate

### CO₂ removal (upgrading)
- Inlet CH₄ (typically 55-70%)
- Outlet CH₄ target: >96% for pipeline, >90% for vehicle
- Methane slip: <2% for most technologies
- Off-gas handling

### Compression
- Inlet pressure: typically 10-30 mbar from digester
- Outlet pressure: 8-10 bar (CHP), 200-250 bar (vehicle fuel)
- Number of stages

## Technology-specific guidance

### Condenser/Chiller (701-CND)
- Shell & tube or plate heat exchanger
- Refrigerant chiller or glycol loop
- Condensate separator
- Automatic drain

### Iron Sponge (710-FE)
- Iron oxide media on wood chips or pellets
- Oxygen dosing for regeneration (2-6% air)
- Bed sizing: gas velocity, contact time
- Media replacement frequency

### Biological Scrubber (710-BIO)
- Packed tower with sulfur-oxidizing bacteria
- Nutrient dosing (N, P)
- Blowdown for sulfur removal
- pH control

### Membrane Upgrading (740-MEM)
- Multiple stage membrane separation
- Permeate (CO₂-rich) vs retentate (CH₄-rich)
- Compression required upstream
- Methane recovery: 98-99.5%

### Flare (750-FLR)
- Open or enclosed design
- Minimum turndown ratio
- Pilot flame and ignition
- Flame arrestor upstream

## What to attach with the datasheet

- Complete biogas analysis
- Gas production rate and variation
- End use specifications
- Site layout for equipment placement
- Permit requirements (emissions, noise)
