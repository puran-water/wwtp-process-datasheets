# Anaerobic Treatment datasheet guidance

Covers: 201-UASB, 201-FFR

## What to decide up front

**1) Reactor type**
- **UASB (Upflow Anaerobic Sludge Blanket)**: high-rate, granular sludge, >2,000 mg/L COD
- **EGSB (Expanded Granular Sludge Bed)**: higher loading than UASB, internal recirculation
- **IC (Internal Circulation)**: two-stage EGSB, very high loading
- **Fixed film/packed bed**: attached growth, lower strength wastewaters
- **Anaerobic lagoon**: low-rate, large footprint, warm climates

**2) Temperature regime**
- **Mesophilic**: 30-38°C, most common, stable operation
- **Thermophilic**: 50-57°C, faster kinetics, more sensitive
- **Ambient**: unheated, tropical/warm climates only

**3) Biogas utilization**
- Flaring only (waste)
- Heat recovery (boiler/engine)
- Power generation (CHP)
- Upgrading to biomethane

## Process data that drives the quote

### Hydraulic design
- Design average flow (m³/h)
- Design peak flow (m³/h)
- Hydraulic retention time: 4-12 hours (UASB), 2-6 hours (EGSB/IC)
- Upflow velocity: 0.5-1.0 m/h (UASB), 2-6 m/h (EGSB)

### Organic loading
- Influent COD (mg/L): typically >2,000 for UASB
- COD loading: 5-15 kg COD/m³/d (UASB), 15-35 kg COD/m³/d (EGSB/IC)
- COD removal efficiency: typically 70-85%
- VFA concentration

### Biogas production
- Theoretical: 0.35 Nm³ CH₄/kg COD removed
- Actual: 0.25-0.30 Nm³ CH₄/kg COD (accounting for losses)
- Biogas composition: typically 65-75% CH₄, 25-35% CO₂
- H₂S concentration (depends on sulfate in feed)

### Nutrient requirements
- COD:N:P ratio requirement (typically 350:5:1)
- Nutrient supplementation if deficient
- Trace metals (Fe, Ni, Co) for methanogenesis

## Mechanical / design interface items

- **Reactor construction**: concrete or steel, gas-tight
- **Influent distribution**: uniform across reactor floor
- **GLS (Gas-Liquid-Solid) separator**: critical for granule retention
- **Biogas collection**: gas dome, piping, safety systems
- **Effluent weirs**: hydraulic control
- **Heating system**: if required (heat exchangers, recirculation)
- **Seed sludge**: source of granular sludge for startup

## Operations considerations

- Startup period: 3-6 months for granule development
- pH control: 6.8-7.4 optimal
- Alkalinity requirements
- Toxicity monitoring (sulfide, heavy metals)
- Granule washout prevention

## What to attach with the datasheet

- Wastewater characterization (COD, VFA, sulfate, nutrients)
- Temperature profile (seasonal)
- Biogas utilization plan
- Effluent post-treatment requirements
