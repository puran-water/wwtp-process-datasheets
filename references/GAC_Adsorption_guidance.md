# GAC (Granular Activated Carbon) Adsorption datasheet guidance

Covers: 330-GAC

## What to decide up front

**1) Application**
- **Taste & odor removal**: drinking water polishing
- **Micropollutant removal**: pharmaceuticals, pesticides, PFAS
- **COD/color removal**: industrial wastewater
- **Dechlorination**: RO pretreatment

**2) Contactor type**
- **Gravity downflow**: most common, simple operation
- **Pressure downflow**: higher loading rates, smaller footprint
- **Upflow (expanded bed)**: for high-solids feeds
- **Pulsed bed**: continuous operation, moving bed

**3) Carbon type**
- **Coal-based**: general purpose, cost-effective
- **Coconut shell**: microporous, good for small molecules
- **Wood-based**: macroporous, good for large molecules/color
- **Specialty carbons**: PFAS-specific, impregnated carbons

**4) Regeneration approach**
- **Virgin carbon replacement**: exchange service, no on-site regen
- **Off-site regeneration**: return spent carbon for thermal regen
- **On-site regeneration**: large installations, thermal or chemical

## Process data that drives the quote

### Hydraulic design
- Design average flow (m³/h)
- Design peak flow (m³/h)
- Empty bed contact time (EBCT): 5-30 minutes typical
- Hydraulic loading: 5-15 m/h (gravity), 10-25 m/h (pressure)

### Contactor sizing
- Bed depth: 1-3 m typical
- Number of vessels: series or parallel configuration
- Vessel diameter/dimensions

### Feed water quality
- Target contaminant(s) and concentrations
- Background organics (TOC, DOC)
- TSS: must be low (<5 mg/L) or pre-filtered
- pH, temperature

### Performance targets
- Effluent contaminant limits
- Breakthrough definition
- Expected carbon life (bed volumes to breakthrough)

### Carbon specifications
- Iodine number: 800-1200 mg/g typical
- Apparent density: 400-550 kg/m³
- Effective size: 0.8-1.2 mm
- Uniformity coefficient: <1.8

## Mechanical / design interface items

- **Vessel construction**: steel or FRP, pressure rating
- **Underdrain system**: nozzles, gravel support, or wedgewire
- **Backwash system**: air scour and water backwash
- **Carbon handling**: fill/removal ports, spent carbon storage
- **Instrumentation**: differential pressure, flow, effluent sampling

## Operations considerations

- Backwash frequency and optimization
- Carbon usage rate tracking
- Breakthrough monitoring (online or grab samples)
- Biological activity in bed (if applicable)

## What to attach with the datasheet

- Water quality analysis (target contaminants + background)
- Isotherm or pilot test data (if available)
- Permit/regulatory requirements
- Carbon supplier options and regeneration services
